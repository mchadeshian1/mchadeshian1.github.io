About Me

Hi Everyone! My name is Mikael Hadeshian, but everyone calls me Miko, and I am currentlya junior majoring in Computer Science and Quantitative Economics at Tufts University just outside Boston. I was born on December 13, 1997 in San Francisco, California but quickly moved accross the bay to Berkeley where I grew up. I graduated from Berkeley High School in 2016. Some peaks included completing the international baccalaureate program and competing in a state championship in basketball.

I grew up in a multi-ethnic household where my mother was Uruguayan and my dad was Armenian. From the countless mate gourds strewn around my house and the strange sounding food like Boureks, that are a staple on my dining room table, to my unwavering support for the Uruguayan national team and the Kardashians, there are signs of my odd heritage everywhere.

These days, when i'm not studying, I love reading books, binge-watching netflix, coding, playing new sports (namely ultimate frisbee), traveling, and spending time with family and friends. I also have enjoyed spending my summers working with young students in the field of educational uplift.
